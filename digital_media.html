Projects

Brochure

NahlaNia

I created a brochure to promote my solo exhibition called "Neither Black Nor White", which features a collection of my fine art photographs. To align with the theme of the collection, I designed the brochure in black and white with a hint of my branding color. The typeface I used for the headings is high-contrast with curves and sharp angles and represents the difference between black and white. To ensure consistency in the design of a bilingual brochure, I took the initiative to create a new Persian typeface that matched the design of the existing English headings. This allowed for a more visually appealing and cohesive design.

Rebranding

TAV

I utilized a purposeful design approach to reimagine TAV's logo, a renovation company, in a way that captures the essence of the brand and its industry focus. The logo is now more responsive to different devices and marketing materials with the addition of a house symbol. By merging these elements, the logo not only retains the familiarity of the brand but also visually reinforces TAV's specialization in transforming living spaces. I also created a style guide that extends across various marketing assets, including business cards, trifold brochures, and T-shirts, where consistent geometries and visual elements are used to create a cohesive and recognizable brand presence.

Branding

Tajhiz Farayand

I took a cohesive design approach in crafting the branding components for Tajhiz Farayand, a startup MedTech company specializing in technical services for high-tech medical equipment. The logo fuses the company's initials into a dynamic arrangement resembling electronic circuits. I integrated the same geometric elements into all designed material to create a unified visual language across all touchpoints. The brand's professionalism, reliability, and forward-thinking are reinforced by its color choices. Navy blue signifies trust and stability, while bright orange represents vibrant energy and dedication to excellence in the industry.

Photography

Label Design

Wine Bottle

In this design challenge, I drew inspiration from the evocative name "Pause Du Soir," which conveys images of tranquil evenings, shared moments, and serene gatherings. I intended to infuse a delicate yet inviting aesthetic into the design, evoking a sense of warmth and tranquility. Through thoughtful consideration of color palette, typography, and illustration, I added a feminine touch to my design to make it more approachable.
